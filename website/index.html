How it works
// Architecture
Why it runs locally

Unlike hosted scanners, PortScan Pro runs entirely on your machine.
The scan packets go directly from your laptop to your network — no middleman.

01

You install the app

A standard Windows installer puts the app in Program Files, adds a Start Menu shortcut, a Desktop icon, and sets it to launch automatically with Windows.

02

It runs silently in your system tray

No terminal window. The app sits in the tray (bottom-right, near the clock). Right-click to open or quit. Your browser points to http://localhost:8000.

03

Scan packets travel from your machine

TCP connections go directly from your laptop to the target. Private IPs (192.168.x.x, 10.x.x.x, 172.x.x.x) are reachable because you are on the same network.

04

Results stream back in real time

Open ports, banners, risk scores, and remediation advice appear live as the scan progresses. Download a PDF report when done.

Why local
🔒

Private IPs work

A hosted server can never reach 192.168.x.x — those are your router and devices. Running locally means the scan happens right on your network.

⚡

Fast & direct

No round-trip to a cloud server. Scan packets travel at LAN speed — 150 threads scanning 1024 ports typically finishes in under 10 seconds.

🛡️

Your data stays local

Scan results, IPs, banners — none of it leaves your machine. The app has no analytics, no telemetry, no account required.

📦

Zero dependencies

Python, Node.js, Docker — none needed. The installer bundles everything. Download, install, done.
